Changed feature: IlcIntMax and IlcIntMin

The constants IlcIntMax and IlcIntMin changed and new constants to represent the smallest and largest integers were introduced.

Users of CP Optimizer Extensions who use the macros IlcIntMax and IlcIntMin should be aware of a change in their definitions.

The constant IlcIntMax is now the largest possible value usable in variables and constraints. On 64-bit operating systems IlcIntMax is the largest positive integer that can be represented as a 64-bit floating point number according to IEEE 754, or the constant 2^53-1. On 32-bit operating systems, this constant is 2^31-1.

The constant IlcIntMin is now the smallest possible value usable in variables and constraints. On 64-bit operating systems, IlcIntMin is the smallest negative integer that can be represented as a 64-bit floating point number according to IEEE 754, or the constant -(2^53-1). On 32-bit operating systems, this constant is -(2^31-1).

The constant IlcIntTop was introduced to represent the largest integer. This constant is the positive integer of largest magnitude representable on the operating system: 2^63-1 on 64-bit operating systems and 2^31-1 on 32-bit operating systems.

Likewise, the constant IlcIntBottom was introduced to represent the smallest integer. This constant is one more than the negative integer of largest magnitude representable on the operating system: -(2^63-1) on 64-bit operating systems and -(2^31-1) on 32-bit operating systems.
