Changes in parameters in CPLEX V12.7.0

These parameters have changed in CPLEX V12.7.0.

In CPLEX V12.7.0, the type of the parameter data consistency checking and modeling assistance has changed. Formerly, the type was Boolean; that is, data checking was simply on or off (one or zero, true or false, depending on your API) in previous versions of CPLEX. Now the type is int, offering users more control over the level of data checking that CPLEX performs.
  • 0 (zero) CPX_DATACHECK_OFF still means for CPLEX to turn off data checking.
  • 1 (one) CPX_DATACHECK_WARN tells CPLEX to turn on data checking and to issue warnings about conditions that it detects during data checking.
  • 2 CPX_DATACHECK_ASSIST tells CPLEX to turn on modeling assistance in addition to data checking. At this setting, CPLEX issues warnings about data consistency and about modeling anomalies, such as disproportionate coefficients, bounds, or righthand side values. This level can also report kappa statistics (also known as the condition number) when it detects ill conditioning in a linear program (LP) or mixed integer program (MIP).

The additional warnings that CPLEX issues during modeling assistance have names that begin CPXMI_. These messages appear on the warning channel or in logs, depending on how you have set messaging and logging in your application. These warnings are available in all CPLEX APIs. See the documentation of these warnings in the reference manual of the Callable Library (C API) in the topic Modeling information in the CPLEX Callable Library (C API) (regardless of your API).
